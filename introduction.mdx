## Welcome to The Incel Test

The Incel Test is a satirical quiz that answers life's most pressing question: **Are you a Chad or a Beta?**

This human-subjects survey was rejected by the Denison University IRB, and for good reason. It evaluates users across two critical dimensions - looks and personality - to place them on a quadrant chart ranging from "Beta Beta" (the worst) to "Chad Chad" (living life on easy mode).

<Note>
  This is a satirical project. The quiz uses deliberately offensive humor and stereotypes for comedic effect.
</Note>

## What Makes This Quiz Unique?

Unlike simple personality quizzes, The Incel Test recognizes that people are multidimensional. The scoring system evaluates:

- **Looks**: Physical attributes like jawline, height, build, facial symmetry, and fashion sense
- **Personality**: Social behaviors, hobbies, relationship history, and lifestyle choices

Your answers determine where you land on a 2D chart with four possible outcomes:

## The Quiz Experience

The quiz consists of **30 questions** split between looks and personality:

- **14 looks questions**: Covering physical attributes from jawline to shoe choice
- **16 personality questions**: Evaluating social life, hobbies, and lifestyle

Each question has weighted importance, with critical questions (like "Have you ever had sex?") carrying 5x the weight of standard questions.

<Info>
  The quiz tracks your answers in real-time, displaying them in a sidebar as you progress through the questions.
</Info>

## Technical Implementation

The Incel Test is built with:

- **Vanilla JavaScript** (`questioner.js`) handling all quiz logic
- **JSON-based questions** (`questions.json`) with 30 questions and weighted scoring
- **Bootstrap 4** for responsive UI
- **SVG chart visualization** showing your final position on the Chad-Beta matrix

See the [How It Works](/how-it-works) page for detailed information about the scoring algorithm and question weighting system.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why does the quiz use two axes?">
    As stated in the FAQ: "lmfao if you think looks or personality are the only thing that matters. People are multidimensional."
    
    The two-axis system allows for more nuanced results than a single score.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How does the scoring work?">
    The quiz uses weighted scoring where each question has a weight (1-5). Answers are scored from -1 to +1, multiplied by the weight, then normalized to fit on a 253x253 pixel chart. See the [How It Works](/how-it-works) page for details.
  </Accordion>
